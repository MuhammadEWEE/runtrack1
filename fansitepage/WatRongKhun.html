    • WAT RONG KHUN

    In Chiang Rai, a province in the far north of Thailand, tourism is inseparable from visiting sacred sites . Wat Rong Khun, opened in 1997, clearly stands out from the crowd: better known to foreigners as the White Temple, this fanciful composition by Chalermchai Kositpipat is one of the essential visits to Chiang Rai during a stay in Thailand . In reality, if the site imitates the appearance of a religious complex, its vocation is quite different: the nine buildings house extraordinary works of art, from statues of meditating Buddha to pop culture idols like Mickael Jackson , to murals that celebrate the doctrines of Theravada Buddhism . Its immaculate white color gives the building a unique character that takes the breath of all visitors, residents and foreigners alike.
